# Axiom To Solo Podcast

## Overview

Turn an `axiom-explainer` Markdown article into a single-host Chinese podcast script. Preserve the article's logical rigor, assumptions, boundaries, positive and negative examples, and reflective ending, while making it sound natural when spoken by one person.

## Workflow

1. Read the source Markdown article and identify:
   - title or topic;
   - core question and conclusion;
   - "求真讲法", "求存讲法", and "思考" sections;
   - named assumptions, applicability boundaries, examples, and references;
   - any uncertain claims or simplifications.
2. Choose a listener hook:
   - Prefer a frequent life/work/study decision problem, recent public topic, or widely felt tension that directly connects to the article's topic.
   - If current news is needed and the article does not provide it, browse or verify before using it. Do not invent "recent hot topics".
   - Keep the hook concrete: a scene, choice, contradiction, or question listeners can immediately recognize.
3. Draft a solo monologue with only `Speaker 1:` labels.
4. Save the result as a `.txt` file under the current project's `markdown/` directory. Create `markdown/` if needed.
5. In the final response, report the saved path and key assumptions. Do not paste the full script unless asked.

## Opening

Start the script exactly with this sentence, then continue into the chosen hook and topic:

```text
Speaker 1: 在快速变化的世界中抓住确定性. hello 大家好我是德哥, 今天的思维筑基我们来聊一聊 : 
```

After the colon, complete the sentence with the topic. Immediately follow with a listener-grabbing problem or timely hook related to the topic.

Example shape:

```text
Speaker 1: 在快速变化的世界中抓住确定性. hello 大家好我是德哥, 今天的思维筑基我们来聊一聊 : <主题>。你有没有发现，<生活问题/热点现象>？表面上看这是<直觉解释>，但今天我们要拆的是更底层的一件事：<核心问题>。
```

## Length

- Target 1,600-2,000 Chinese characters for normal-speed narration.
- Hard limit 2,200 Chinese characters unless the user explicitly asks for a longer version.
- Normal-speed narration must stay under 8 minutes.
- Cut diagrams, long citations, formal proof details, and repetitive examples. Keep the reasoning chain and boundaries.

## Structure

Follow this order unless the article clearly needs a different flow:

1. Fixed opening sentence plus hook: use a related daily problem or verified current topic.
2. Topic promise: tell listeners what one mental model they will get by the end.
3. Story or scene: give one concrete scenario that motivates the concept.
4. 求真: explain what the axiom/theorem/viewpoint actually says, where it comes from, and which assumptions support it.
5. First suspense point: state a common mistake and say it will be resolved after the mechanism is clear.
6. Mechanism: convert the article's diagram/proof/causal chain into spoken reasoning.
7. 求存: show how the idea transfers into study, work, technology, business, money, time, collaboration, or decision-making.
8. Positive example: show what changes when the assumptions hold.
9. Second suspense point: warn that the same method fails when one named assumption is false.
10. Negative example and boundary: explain the failure through the broken assumption, not vague personal error.
11. 思考: give a deeper question, counterfactual, or cross-domain connection.
12. Closing recap: leave 3 repeatable takeaways in spoken form.

## Script Requirements

- Use only `Speaker 1:` as the speaker label.
- Write natural spoken Chinese, not Markdown prose.
- Do not include title blocks, timestamps, music cues, stage directions, bullet lists, tables, Mermaid, SVG, or references inside the script.
- Preserve important English terms, names, formulas, and source concepts from the article when they matter.
- Define specialized terms the first time they appear.
- Distinguish motivation, proof or derivation, assumptions, and practical boundary. Do not call an axiom "proved" inside its own system.
- Keep rigor by naming the assumption behind every major conclusion.
- Keep story by using scenes, contrast, and consequence, not decorative anecdotes.
- Keep hooks honest: no exaggerated fear, fake news, invented data, or unsupported claims.
- Use short sentences and clear signposts such as "先说结论", "关键在这里", "这就是第一个坑", "反过来看", and "最后你只要记住三句话".

## Retention Devices

Place 3-5 "埋点" throughout the script:

- Opening curiosity gap: "表面上是 A，其实底层是 B。"
- Early promise: "听完你会多一个判断工具。"
- Before mechanism: "真正容易误判的地方在这里。"
- Before boundary: "但这个方法有一个前提，一旦它不成立，结论会反过来。"
- Before closing: "如果只能带走一句话，就是..."

Make each 埋点 advance the explanation. Do not add empty suspense.

## Quality Checks

Before finishing, verify:

- The script starts exactly with the required opening sentence after `Speaker 1:`.
- The opening hook is tied to the topic and is either everyday-observable or verified if it references current events.
- Only `Speaker 1:` appears.
- The script is under 2,200 Chinese characters unless otherwise requested.
- The article's core conclusion, assumptions, boundary, positive example, negative example, and reflective question are preserved.
- No diagrams, Markdown tables, citation lists, production notes, or unsupported new facts remain in the script.
- The output is saved under `./markdown/` as a `.txt` file.
